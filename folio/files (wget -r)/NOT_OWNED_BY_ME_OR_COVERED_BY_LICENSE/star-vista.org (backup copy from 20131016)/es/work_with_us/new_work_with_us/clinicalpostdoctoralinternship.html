Job Description: Clinical Postdoctoral Internship

Program/ Department: Women’s Enrichment Center (WEC)

Salary/Wage: Stipend Internship, $13/hour, with Health Benefits, Vacation, Sick and Holiday Benefit

Status: Full-time, Non-Exempt – Internship (1 Year Commitment) Starts 4/29/2013

PROGRAM DESCRIPTION: WEC is an intensive day treatment program for women uniquely designed to address substance abuse and mental health issues with a strong focus on the treatment of trauma. Clients attend WEC for 5 hours a day, 5 days a week for a minimum of 3 months before completing the program. WEC provides group and individual therapy and case management to link clients with resources relating to housing, job skills, finances, parenting, family therapy, and medical and dental needs. Daily psychoeducational and process therapy groups address topics including recovery, trauma, emotional regulation, health and wellness, parenting, and other life skills. We use a wide range of modalities to provide holistic, strength-based treatment.

Interns provide individual therapy, group therapy and case management with the opportunity to also provide family therapy. Applicants need to be comfortable working at an intensive day treatment program with clients with challenging issues, liaising with a client’s multi-disciplinary team and working as part of a team at WEC. Ideally an intern would have experience working with the severely mentally ill including working with trauma.

WEC staff work closely together as a team. Interns would gain in depth experience of working with complex client cases in a very supportive environment. They will have the opportunity to develop group therapy skills and to work with both highly motivated clients and clients mandated to treatment. In addition to the required 1 hour individual supervision each week with a psychologist, interns receive 2 hours each week of doctoral group supervision and 2 hours each week of case consultation at WEC. Interns have access to the full range of San Mateo County trainings, which include trainings on for example Law and Ethics, Motivational Interviewing, Suicide Prevention, Trauma-informed care, and anxiety treatment. Interns can attend StarVista’s one-way mirror training. Interns will gain experience with resources available in San Mateo County, Medi-Cal paperwork requirements, and Avatar (the San Mateo county electronic medical record system).

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS: Clinical Psychology Doctorate (Ph.d or Psy.D) or close to graduating, eligible to be a Registered Psychologist or would soon be eligible, team player, group therapy experience, available Monday to Friday daytime hours, able to pass fingerprint clearance.

PREFERED QUALIFICATIONS: Bilingual Spanish, experience working with substance abuse, trauma and the severely mentally ill. Lived experience of trauma or substance use. Familiarity with San Mateo County resources and experience liaising with a client’s multi-disciplinary team. Medi-Cal and Avatar experience.

TO APPLY: To apply, please send a cover letter and resume to Charlotte Ormond at charlotte.ormond@star-vista.org.
